## The score

Every permit package the Ashlr product assembles ships with a calibrated
**approval probability** — a number between 0 and 1 representing the
likelihood the AHJ approves the submission without a correction notice.

It's the single most useful number in the workflow. Installers fix the issues
that cost them the most expected probability before submitting; reviewers
prioritize submissions least likely to bounce.

## How it's computed

Two layers:

1. **Deterministic compliance pass.** Every AHJ rule is evaluated against the
   project. Severity weights:
    - `blocking`: -50% per issue
    - `likely_rejection`: -15% per issue
    - `advisory`: -2% per issue
   Floored at 5%, ceilinged at 98%.

2. **LLM judgment overlay.** A reasoning-tier model reviews ambiguous
   conditions the deterministic pass can't catch (PE stamp jurisdiction,
   roof-pathway interpretation, etc.). It nudges the score up or down with
   a written rationale.

## Calibration

We log every (probability, outcome) pair. Calibration plots run quarterly.
If the score over- or under-predicts in a given AHJ by more than ±10%, the
rule weights for that AHJ are tuned and a regression test pinned.

## What it doesn't capture

- Reviewer mood, queue depth, jurisdictional politics.
- Last-minute portal outages.
- Anything the homeowner adds after submission.

The score is decision support, not a guarantee. The product is structured
so that a 50% score still produces a fully usable submission package — it
just flags what to fix first.
