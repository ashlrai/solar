# AHJ Teardown: Fairfax County, VA

*First in a weekly series. We break down one US jurisdiction's solar permitting process — what's required, where it breaks, and what we'd change. Written for installers and by an AI + humans who submit permits here.*

---

## TL;DR

- **Turnaround**: ~10 business days (median) for a clean residential PV application.
- **Portal**: Fairfax County Land Development Services online portal.
- **Pain**: VA-licensed PE structural letter is a recurring bottleneck — third-party PE networks introduce 2–5 extra business days.
- **Gotcha**: IRC R324.7 roof access pathways are stricter than state minimums. ~18% of first submissions get kicked on setback diagrams (our internal estimate).
- **SolarAPP+**: Not currently eligible.

## Required documents

1. Application form (county-specific)
2. Site plan with array layout
3. Single-line electrical diagram
4. Structural letter (VA-licensed PE, stamped)
5. Module + inverter + racking datasheets
6. Placard / labeling spec

## Fees
- Base residential PV: $120 (verify via portal at submission).

## Top three rejection reasons

1. **Missing PE stamp on structural letter** — 34% of rejections we see have this root cause.
2. **Non-compliant roof access pathway diagram** — IRC R324.7 requires 36" ridge pathway on hipped roofs with array. Diagrams often mark 30".
3. **Busbar / 120% rule calculations missing or incorrect** — NEC 705.12 gets waved at but not calculated on the single-line.

## What we'd change (installer-side)

1. **Pre-flight a PE letter template** specific to your most common racking + roof type combos. Reuse aggressively.
2. **Add a dimensioned roof-access diagram to your site plan** — don't rely on verbal annotation.
3. **Run a 705.12 check at design time, not review time.** This is the #1 unforced error.
