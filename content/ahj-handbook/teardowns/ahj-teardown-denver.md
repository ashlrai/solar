# AHJ Teardown: Denver (CCD + Xcel Energy)

*Eighth in our weekly AHJ teardown series. Denver is one of the fastest residential solar AHJs in the country — when the structural calcs are right. When they're not, it's a 4-week round trip.*

---

## TL;DR

- **Turnaround**: ~5 business days under SolarAPP+ for clean residential PV.
- **Portal**: Denver Community Planning + Development; Xcel Energy interconnection in parallel.
- **Pain**: **Snow load** (40 psf ground snow in most of Denver county) trips up out-of-state PE letters that under-spec for ASCE 7-22 Cs/Ce/Ct factors. Hipped roofs in foothill neighborhoods routinely need >40 psf design.
- **Gotcha**: Xcel net metering tier matters. Above 25 kW the application path changes; above 120% of annual usage you can't net-meter at retail.

## What works in Denver
- SolarAPP+ approval same day on standard residential PV.
- DCDPD plan reviewers respond fast (median 2 business days on first cycle).
- Net metering at retail rate up to system size capped at 120% of historical usage.

## Top rejection reasons
1. **Snow load under-spec** — out-of-state PEs default to 30 psf ground snow; Denver code requires 40+ psf with drift coefficients on hipped roofs. ~35% of first submissions.
2. **Xcel interconnection app missing or filed sequentially** — adds 2–4 weeks of dead time after the building permit clears.
3. **Roof tilt + snow shed pathways** — Denver Fire wants 36" pathways even on shallow-pitch roofs in fire-prone foothill zip codes.

## What we'd change (installer-side)
1. **CO PE on retainer** — Denver-specific snow + wind loads are reflexive for in-state PEs. Out-of-state stamps usually need a revision.
2. **File Xcel concurrent**, never sequential. The Xcel app fields are minimal but the queue is real.
3. **Pre-design rooftop pathway check** for foothills and fire-zone parcels — bake the 36" envelope in before BOM.

## What Ashlr automates for Denver
- ASCE 7-22 snow + drift load calculation pre-filled per parcel zip code.
- Xcel Energy net metering package generated alongside CCD building permit.
- Parcel-level fire-zone overlay check; surfaces 36" pathway requirement at design time.
- Net-metering eligibility pre-check (system size vs annual usage).
