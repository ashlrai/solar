# AHJ Teardown: New York City (DOB + FDNY)

*Fifth in our weekly AHJ teardown series. NYC is among the toughest jurisdictions for residential solar in the country — dual-agency review, nine-foot perimeter clearance, ASCE 7 retrofit math on pre-war buildings, Con Edison in parallel. Here's the map.*

---

## TL;DR

- **Turnaround**: ~28 business days median. P90 past two months if FDNY bounces the array geometry.
- **Portal**: NYC DOB NOW · Build + FDNY Solar Unit (distinct workflows, no unified submission).
- **Pain**: **9-foot FDNY perimeter clearance** reshapes the array on a third of sites. Out-of-state PEs aren't accepted. Con Edison interconnection runs in parallel with its own SLA.
- **Gotcha**: Pre-1961 buildings often need ASCE 7 seismic + wind upgrade analysis that in-state PEs bake in; out-of-staters miss it and bounce.

## The two-agency problem

NYC residential PV runs through both:
1. **NYC Department of Buildings (DOB)** — structural + electrical plan review
2. **FDNY Solar Unit** — rooftop access + firefighter-safety clearances

Neither agency signs off until the other does. Approval on one side is not transferable; each has its own correction loop.

## The 9-foot clearance trap

FDNY requires a **9-foot perimeter clearance** on most residential roofs with PV. That's dramatically stricter than the 36" pathway rules elsewhere. On a small Brooklyn row-house roof, 9 feet of clearance can remove 30–40% of your array.

Out-of-state installers designing to CFC 1205 defaults reliably lose the first submission here. We pre-flight FDNY geometry at design time.

## Top rejection reasons
1. **9-foot clearance not met** on one or more edges — ~27% of first submissions.
2. **PE stamp from another state** — automatic kick. NY requires a NY-licensed PE.
3. **Structural calcs missing ASCE 7 seismic/wind retrofit** on pre-war buildings — ~18%.
4. **Con Edison interconnection letter of intent absent** from the DOB package — loses 5–10 days.

## What we'd change (installer-side)
1. **Design FDNY-first**. Start with the 9-foot envelope, fit the array to what remains.
2. **Partner with a NY-licensed PE** early. Do not attempt to run an out-of-state stamp.
3. **File Con Edison in parallel** with DOB submission. Never sequential.
4. **ASCE 7 check mandatory** on any pre-1961 building — bake it into your pre-design checklist.
