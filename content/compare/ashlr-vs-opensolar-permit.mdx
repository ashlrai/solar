OpenSolar ships usable permit docs out of the design suite on a freemium model.
Like Aurora, it's generation not evaluation — no AHJ intelligence, no
submission, no plan-check automation.

## At a glance

| Attribute | Ashlr | OpenSolar permit output |
|---|---|---|
| Pricing | $199 / permit or $299 / seat | Free + usage fees |
| Design quality | We don't do design — OpenSolar's is great | Top-tier |
| AHJ rule evaluation | Yes · 55 AHJs codified | No |
| Plan-check response | Agent drafts in minutes | Manual |
| Integration | Webhook + API into OpenSolar | — |

## When OpenSolar wins

Design + proposals + customer signing flow. That's their strength. They
deliberately don't compete on AHJ submission.

## When Ashlr wins

You finish the OpenSolar proposal and want permits to stop being the next 3
weeks. Our [OpenSolar integration](/integrations/opensolar) fires on
`proposal.accepted` and pulls the design through our pipeline.

## Stack pattern

OpenSolar (free) + Ashlr ($199/permit) is the cheapest US installer stack
that gets you from cold lead to filed permit, end-to-end. Total per-permit cost
delta vs Aurora ($350/user/month for design pack) + Greenlancer ($700/permit):
**$351 saved per permit at OpenSolar+Ashlr volumes**.
