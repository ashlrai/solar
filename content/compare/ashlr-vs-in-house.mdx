title: "Ashlr vs. an in-house permit coordinator"
description: "Hire a coordinator at $60–90k/year, or process the same volume for $199 per permit."
---

The status quo at most mid-sized installers. A full-time coordinator runs AHJ
paperwork, chases plan-check, follows up with utilities. Effective but hits a
per-head ceiling.

## At a glance

| Attribute | Ashlr | In-house coordinator |
|---|---|---|
| Cost | $199 / permit | $60–90k / year + benefits |
| Throughput | Unlimited · scales with compute | 150–250 permits / year / coordinator |
| AHJ coverage learning | Centralized knowledge graph | Tribal knowledge per hire |
| Night / weekend | Yes | No |
| Redundancy | Multi-provider LLM failover | One person, zero redundancy |

## When the coordinator wins

Novel / complex jurisdictions not in the knowledge graph, customer calls + field
walks that need a human. They're not going away.

## When Ashlr wins

Routine permits should never touch a human. Your coordinator handles **3× more**
permits when Ashlr does the rote work — AHJ classification, package assembly,
plan-check drafting. They focus on the genuinely-hard 20% of submissions.

## Math

At 200 permits/year:

| | Ashlr only | In-house only | Both (recommended) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Coordinator cost | — | $80k | $80k |
| Per-permit cost | $39,800 | — | $19,900 (50% offload) |
| Total | **$39,800** | $80,000 | $99,900 |
| Permits/year capacity | unlimited | 200 (ceiling) | 600+ |

The "both" pattern is what works at 100+ installs/year. Ashlr handles routine,
the coordinator handles edge cases + relationship work, total throughput triples
at modest incremental cost.
